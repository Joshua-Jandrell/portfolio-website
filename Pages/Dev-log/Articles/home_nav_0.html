Home naviation section 11 April 2022 The home section will be dedicated to showcasing featured articles on my site. This could be my newest blog entry or a recently made game. This will be the first section on the index page and the point that all 'home' links redirect to. That 'hello world' thing is nice and all but it would be a pain to have to scroll though every time.

So how will this all work?

Naturally these will be a selection of articles which I think are great and that I want the visitor to read. If the visitor want's to look at my work this is a great think to point them to my best work without putting it on a pedestal. (I mean it kinda is on a pedestal - but it's a gentle nudge rather than a violent push.)

This isn't really about navigation: it's about showing the visitor that I'm busy with a bunch of interesting projects. This enforces the idea that they should continue exploring the site because it has a lot to offer. It also makes me look engaged and interesting as a designer.

If a particular article catches the visitors attention, it will open in a new tab. This encourages them to return to the home page and keep exploring.

The caption 'explore' is a call to action which should encourage victors to follow the links.

How will this be structured?


This will be a simple grid. A gird layout is less hierarchal than a stacked list and gives the impression of variety and organisation. Grid provide strcture and order which make the site appeare well constructed and solidly put togerther.

The articles will be represented by large icons to make clicking easy. Each article will have a short preview to create intrigue. The layout is simple and minimal so that the visitor can take the site in without feeling overwhelmed.
