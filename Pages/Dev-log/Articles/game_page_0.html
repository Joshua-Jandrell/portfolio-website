Structure for game pages 14 April 2022 Each game will have a short article written about it to provide more information about what's going on before the visitor starts playing. This context will help them understand what the game is trying to do.

How will this be structured?


A short description about the game is very important because many of my games were made with limited time and I wasn't able to do everything I wanted. I need to explain what I was trying to do and what I was able to do. I will try keep this frank, breif and to the point so that visitors can get on with playing. This section is placed above the download section to encourage the viewer to read about my game first and understand the context before they play it.

However, the download section should be prominent and distinctly formatted; making easy for one to download and play the game. That's what they are here for after all.

This is followed by a credits section. Giving credits to myself and acknowledging the contribution of others is simply good practice - especially on a portfolio site. This may also allow me to link to my colleagues' websites in the future which is pretty cool!

Back button encourages the viewer to easily return to the homepage and engage with more content.

Share and feedback buttons are to encourage further engagement. I'd love my games to get exposure or learn how I could improve If the viewer does either its very beneficial to me. These are at the bottom because I assume most users would like to experience the content before they wish to talk about it.

Images of gameplay will be placed down the right-hand side to give the visitor a preview of what's in the game without being intrusive or distracting. I considered videos but I'd rather people played my games to experience them for themselves.
