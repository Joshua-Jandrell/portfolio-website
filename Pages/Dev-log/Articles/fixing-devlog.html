Fixing the dev-log 24 May 2022

Every entry added to an unoptimized dev-log is an entry wasted. As it stands it’s a monolithic block of html and I’m sick of it. It’s hard to work on, tricky to navigate and impossible to maintain. . I know I’m going to redo this whole thing, It’s just a matter of time.

And that time is now!

What’s the problem?

There are several:

  1. Firstly: The left-hand index is not scrollable and gives users no indication of where they are in the dev-log.
  2. Secondly: Sections pop open allowing users to read more articles. This is fine if the reader starts at the top and moves down. But what if they want to move up the log? They currently need to scroll all the way though the article they just read which is hardly conveniently.
  3. Thirdly: Things could be more fluid. The details/summary tags are the correct approach; however they jump open suddenly which might confuse the user if they follow a link to a section that remains cloased then they get there. What if they think there is no more content to be found?
  4. Finally: The log is getting cumbersome and tedious to manage. The html is long and only growing. Even simple tasks like setting up index links can be tedious. Ideally, I would automat the entire thing – but I have no backend on Git-hub pages. I think I can still make my life easier than it is now.

Now that I know what's wrong, it's time to start fixing.
