Re-re-reboot 18 April 2022

Up until quite recently most of the dev-log has been scattered across disparate document. Some of it was interesting. Some of it was hot garbage. Most of it will be lost to time.

I'm currently in the process of adding the relevant entries here. This is a short refection of some other the prominent previous iterations of the site. (I really do faff a lot).

So you're telling me you're just leaving half the delog out?

Yes. Because it's irrelevant. Many of the old entries related to abandoned designs. Typing them all out would be tedious and quite unnecessary. Why should I explaining why I did what I decided not to do.

Because it informs what comes next?

Okay. True. It does. But I think I'll be easier if I summarise it. Instead of giving the excruciating details I am prone to provide.

Without further ado; here are some of my abandoned ideas:

The Sunrise

This website was originally going to be styled to look like a sunrise… with each page based on the sky.


When visitors first landed on my homepage, they would be greeted by a night sky. As they scrolled down, a glowing sun would rise and illuminate the page. A beautiful first impression! After that each page would have a sunrise background to continue the motif.

This is the first website I've ever made - sunrises speak to the idea of new beginnings. An open sky invokes the idea of freedom and limitless possibilities. I also love watching the sunrise, I find it calming and relaxing.

Furthermore, the sunrise let me use an awesome orange and blue colour scheme. The vibrant and energetic orange symbolised energy and enthusiasm while blue tempered this with a calm and trustworthy tones. Orange and blue are also contrasting colours so suing they could really make each-other pop (or clash horribly if done wrong).


It was convoluted and the code I was using was a mess. It was becoming a pain to implement. It wasn't looking that great either. I was new to html and the site was crippled by a misshapn skeleton. It dawned on me that while the sunrise spoke to my experience of making the website it was completely unrelated to my skills as an engineer and game designer. I felt that visitors to the site would have not idea what it meant, what I did or what was going on.

In hindsight this was not a bad idea. (I was even tempted to revert to it. The git branch for this reboot is even called 'back-to-sky'). It had the potential to be very aesthetically pleasing and was filled with a lot of good ideas.

The 'hello' world into screen and final colour scheme were both cannibalised form this iteration. As was my choice to use simple and readable body text.

Star gazing

This was a haphazard branch of the sunrise theme, originally intended to be the design form my dev-log. I had the idea of arranging elements on my website in constellations. I wanted exploring the site to feel like looking though the night sky invoking awe and wonder.


Setting something like this up is hard and I was trying to show off with skills I didn't have. (Funny enough I think I could pull it off now, but I know I shouldn't). This iteration had all the bad parts of the sky one. The design rationale was essentially: “it looks cool, and I like space” The consultations were messy and hard to code. I stopped working on the site and lost motivation.

Bluprints

An ironic theme for a failed plan. This time round the idea had reason and logic to it. Blueprints speak to creativity and design. They reflect technical capability and look awful.


To be fair they could've looked good, but I didn't have a defined style guid, just a vague idea. As a result, I potted around with 'blue-print-like' styling. I wanted the site to look cutting edge but instead it looked like it came from the 90's.


Again, not a bad idea, but horrid execution. Even now I am shuddering. My mind is scarred with 'error screen blue'.

The wireframes for this iteration however where great and I recycled some of their concepts for the final build. You may recognise the dev-log timeline.

Rainbows

This was the next notable idea I had, and it formed the framework for the site you are on right now. I'd begun to realize a pattern with my failed sites. The ideas were great, the rational was decent and the implementation was hideous. Maybe my ideas were too big. Maybe I was giving up to early. Maybe I wasn't focused. Most likely it was all the above.

A lot of the style and structure details for rainbows have been implemented here, so you'll see plenty abridged versions of my rambling in this log. Rainbows was less about styling and more about a philosophy.

This time round I was focused on what the site said and how it felt, rather than how it looked. I tried to be minimal in my approach to avoid technical pit falls and I planned less and did more.

All my previous work had been subject to meticulous planning and over-design. I spent hours saying what I was going to do and then doing nothing. Rainbows meant to be more hands on. I defined a core theme and style and then I was to set about building a page that would fit it. Little by little, line by line.

Importunely I'm still prone to massive deliberation. CSS is a deep rabbit-hole and sematic markup is a bottomless pit. I stubbled down both on my occasion along with an inability to make simple design choices without 5000 revisions.

I still need to learn how to move on and say good enough is good enough. But I am leaning, and I will get there.


The only major feature I dropped from rainbows was the colour-scheme. I used all colours of the rainbow to make the site look vibrant and colourful. This destroyed all legibility and compositional unity. All the colours were just a bit too much.
