Revised style guild 16 April 2022 This site has undergone quite a few stylistic changes during development, but it's been finalised now. Here is the official style guid.

Aesthetic

I am primarily a systems designer be that games, engineering, or programming. Art and graphics are not my forte; thus, I will not be using illustrations and images as the focal point of the site. The site itself is the artefact of most importance to my future employers: if I cannot make a good website how could their trust me to construct their system.

A sleek minimal athletic is used to make the site feel focused, polished, and professional. This will showcase the site as a system rather than a piece of visual art.

Solid lines and square boxes (without rounded edges) will be used to provide a sense of order and structure. Solid boxes appear trustworthy and confident. This should make visitors feel that the site is well constructed, well organised, and reliable.

Tone

Although the sites construction appears to be formal the site itself should not be. It is important that I still come across as a creative, energetic, and artistic individual because these are strengths of mine too. The site cannot be too business-like. It should feel fun and interesting rather than a soulless repository of work for future employers. This can be achieved with a causal writing style, the use of contraction and slang and a conversational tone.

Colour pallet

The predominant colour of the site is white. White as a background colour will make the site feel clean and unclutters. This helps to create a sense of minimalism and clarity. This Black text on a white background feels natural and is easy to read - delivering simple and comfortable experience is important because it makes the system appear to be of a higher quality.

Orange is used for decorative elements and highlights. Orange is bright and ethicistic. It suggests energy and excitement which helps to convey the more fun-loving site to myself and my website. Orange is also often use to enrage actions such as purchases and is assisted with value. Being seen as valuable or a 'good deal' is useful when trying to sell oneself.

Light blue is used as a secondary highlight colour. Blue is seen as relaxed, reliable, and thoughtful. The light blue helps to create a casual atmosphere in conjunction with the orange.

Blue and orange are contrasting colours and thus appear stringer and brighter when used together. Their combination gives a sense of balance, organisation and harmony. This represents a well-tuned site and my personality, with a balance of creativity (orange) and technical capability (blue).

The bright colour platelet gives the site a vibrant presence and a playful energy, but it might lack presence if it were not anchored with a darker tone. Addressing a third hue might make the site feel to busy and could cause the contrasting blue and orange to clash with each other. Thus, black is used to maintain a simple palette and a minimal aesthetic.

Font types and decoration

Monospace will be used for headings. Monospace is often used in programming and technical applications. It is ideal to suggest to the viewer that I work in this space and write code. Headings are decorated with double forward slashes and square brackets (simple symbols often used while programming) to enhance this effect.

It is important that visitors feel comfortable on the site and find it easy to engage with articles. Arial is used for the main body text. This font is familiar and easy to read. As a sans-serif, Arial is modern and less informal (without being unprofessional). This augments the well-made but playful appearance of the site.
